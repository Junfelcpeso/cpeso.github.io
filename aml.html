Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy

  1. 1. Introduction

    CPESO is committed to preventing and detecting money laundering activities in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. This Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy outlines the measures and procedures in place to mitigate the risk of money laundering and ensure regulatory compliance.

  2. 2. Compliance Officer

    The CPESO Compliance Officer is responsible for overseeing and implementing the AML policy. The Compliance Officer will ensure that all employees are trained and aware of their responsibilities to prevent and report suspicious activities.

  3. 3. Risk Assessment

    CPESO conducts regular risk assessments to identify and assess the money laundering risks associated with its products, services, customers, and geographical locations.

  4. 4. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

    4.1 Identification and Verification

    CPESO will implement procedures for the identification and verification of customer identities.

    4.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

    The level of due diligence will be commensurate with the assessed risk of the customer and transaction.

  5. 5. Customer Risk Profiling

    CPESO categorizes customers based on their risk profile. High-risk customers undergo more stringent due diligence measures.

  6. 6. Transaction Monitoring

    CPESO employs systems and procedures to monitor transactions for unusual or suspicious activities. Thresholds and criteria for triggering investigations are clearly defined.

  7. 7. Reporting Suspicious Activities

    7.1 Internal Reporting

    Employees are obligated to report any suspicious activities to the designated point of contact.

    The designated contact will conduct investigations and escalate to the Compliance Officer if necessary.

    7.2 External Reporting

    Will comply with all legal requirements for reporting suspicious activities to relevant authorities.

  8. 8. Employee Training

    Provides regular training to employees on AML policies, procedures, and their roles in preventing money laundering.

  9. 9. Record Keeping

    CPESO maintains records of customer information, transactions, and due diligence documentation for the prescribed period as required by regulations.

  10. 10. Internal Controls and Monitoring

    CPESO implements internal controls to ensure compliance with AML policies. Regular monitoring and auditing are conducted to identify and rectify any weaknesses.

  11. 11. Periodic Review and Updating

    CPESO regularly reviews and updates the AML policy to reflect changes in regulations, business operations, and the risk landscape.

  12. 12. Communication

    This AML policy is communicated to all relevant stakeholders, including employees, customers, and regulators.

  13. 13. Legal Compliance

    CPESO seeks legal advice to ensure that the AML policy complies with applicable laws and regulations.
